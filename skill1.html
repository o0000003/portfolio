college project.

The movie poster for "Lab Animal" shows the narrative of innocent lab animals who are suffering with chemicals every day to meet human desires, as shown in a horrible movie poster done with exceptional Photoshop skills.

The movie poster depicts an area where animals are subjected to high levels of chemicals for cosmetic purposes. The poster portrays an animal that has grown massive and aggressive as a result of chemical overdose tests, which is displayed in a gloomy setting to enhance the poster's horrific aspect. It is crashing into the research center, causing disaster and attacking the scientists.

The poster conveys a strong message: animals are friends, not experiments. "Lab Animal" challenges us to consider the harmful impacts of using animals for experimentation.

In crafting this captivating project, I embarked on a journey of digital transformation, seamlessly merging two distinct images to create compelling compositions. For the first scene, I carefully selected a striking image of a snowy forest as the backdrop, setting the stage for the mystical encounter. With precision and finesse, I blended the noble head of a dog with the sleek body of a fox, ensuring a seamless integration that evokes a sense of wonder and harmony. In the second composition, I juxtaposed the curious gaze of a chameleon with the unexpected form of a pickle resting on a plate. Through meticulous layering and blending techniques, I brought these contrasting elements together, infusing the scene with whimsy and charm.
